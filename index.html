    About Me

    Hi there, I am Chonghao Zhang (Chinese name: å¼ ç¿€æ˜Š), you can call me "Matthew". I am currently a dual-degree Ph.D. candidate jointly supervised by the Urban Mobility Institute at Tongji University under the supervision of Prof. Xiao Luo, and the Faculty of Engineering at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University under the supervision of Prof. Jingzheng Ren. I previously minored in Finance at the School of Economics, Fudan University (2021.09 â€“ 2023.10).

    My research interests center on urban computing to reveal complex human mobility and social behavioral patterns. Specifically, I focus on:

    • Mechanisms underlying complex spatiotemporal and social behavioral patterns in human activities
    • Agent-based modeling (ABM) simulation of human behavior and policy intervention assessment
    • Urban resilience mechanisms shaped by adaptive human behavior
    • Differential pollutant exposure across populations and environmental justice under extreme weather conditions

    My research aims to understand the generative mechanisms and spatiotemporal propagation dynamics of urban and social events, thereby gaining deeper insights into the interaction mechanisms between human activities and urban systems. This work serves to advance social sensing, urban sustainable development, fine-grained urban management, and evidence-based policymaking.

    I am always open to collaborations. You can find my publications on Google Scholar, and feel free to reach out to me at MatthewZhang_CH@outlook.com.
